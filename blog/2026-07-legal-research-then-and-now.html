Mauritian Legal Research: From the Law Library to AI

Ask a senior member of the Mauritian bar how they researched in the 1990s and you will hear about bound volumes of the Mauritius Reports, photocopied judgments passed between chambers, and the institutional memory of colleagues who simply remembered the case you needed. It worked — because it had to — but it made research a scarce resource rationed by seniority and time.

The digitisation half-step

The 2000s and 2010s brought online access to legislation and judgment databases. Retrieval got faster, but the intellectual work didn't change: you still had to know what to search for, read everything the keyword net dragged in, and assemble the synthesis yourself. Keyword search finds documents; it does not answer questions. For a bilingual, hybrid jurisdiction — where the concept you need may be phrased in French in the Code and in English in the judgment applying it — keywords miss precisely the connections that matter.

What actually changed with AI

The current generation of tools inverts the workflow. Instead of retrieving documents for you to synthesise, systems like Themis perform the synthesis across the entire corpus — every Act, regulation, and judgment, in both languages — and hand you the answer with the sources attached. Three things follow:

  • The junior's afternoon becomes four minutes — the mechanical sweep is no longer where careers are spent.
  • Completeness stops depending on stamina. The 19th-century prescription authority and last month's Privy Council decision surface in the same pass.
  • Verification replaces excavation as the practitioner's core research act: you check the cited sources rather than hunt for them.

What didn't change

Judgment. Which authority to lead with, how to distinguish the awkward case, what to concede, how to advise a client whose problem is only partly legal — none of that moved an inch. The bar's craft was never really the finding; it was always what you did with what you found. The tools have finally caught up with that truth.
